Research

My research focuses on machine learning, domain generalization, and multimodal representation learning. Below are my research projects and publications.

Contrastive Learning Under Domain Shift: Do Positive Pairs Help or Hurt Robustness?

Meghana Sunil

In Progress | Advisor: Dr. Joe Dhanith

Investigating how positive pairs in contrastive learning frameworks (SimCLR, MoCo-v2, BYOL) impact robustness across domain shifts. Analyzing representation quality under domain generalization scenarios and developing novel pair formulations to improve cross-domain generalization while preserving representation quality.

Meghana Sunil

In Progress | Advisor: Dr. Joe Dhanith

Graph-Guided Cross-Modal Representation Learning of EEG–fNIRS via Spatio-Temporal Transformers for Stress Analysis

Meghana Sunil

Submitted: Computer Methods and Programs in Biomedicine | Advisor: Dr. Jeetashree Aparajeeta

Proposed CM-GGT, a cross-modal gated fusion framework integrating a spectrogram-transformer EEG encoder and graph neural network fNIRS encoder. Achieved 93.2% accuracy and 0.967 AUC on simultaneous EEG–fNIRS recordings, outperforming 11 state-of-the-art baselines.

Meghana Sunil

IEEE Access | Advisor: Dr. Natarajan B

Self-Attentive Quantile GAN with Causally Grounded Machine Learning for Vocal Biomarker Discovery in Parkinson's Disease Detection

Meghana Sunil

IEEE Access | Advisor: Dr. Nachiyappan S

Developed a novel framework integrating causal inference with voice analysis, leveraging vocal frequency spread as a key biomarker for Parkinson's disease detection. Achieved 95.03% precision and an AUC-ROC of 97.84%, outperforming traditional machine learning models while improving interpretability.
